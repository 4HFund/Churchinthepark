The Message

Go Find Your One

Pastor Bobby Alan Westfall
Most religions tell humanity to search for God. The Gospel tells us God searched for humanity first. From Genesis to the cross, God always moves first. You may think you wandered into this morning — but what if God has been pursuing you all along?
The Shepherd left ninety-nine to go after one. Heaven doesn't count crowds — it counts names. You have never been 'another broken person' to God. He knows your name, your story, and every tear you've cried that no one else has seen.
The sheep contributed nothing to its own rescue. It didn't earn the shepherd's help or promise to do better. It simply allowed itself to be carried. Christianity isn't about climbing your way to God — it's about a God who came down to carry you. Jesus carried a cross so He could carry us.
The coin never stopped being silver because it was covered in dust. Sin damages us, distorts us, and separates us — but it never changes the fact that you were created by God and loved by God. Before anyone ever labeled you a failure, God called you image-bearer. He isn't looking because He forgot where you are. He's looking because He refuses to leave you where you are.
The father in the story saw his son from a long way off — because he had never stopped watching. He ran, threw his arms around him, and kissed him before his son could even finish his rehearsed speech. That is the heart of God toward you. While you were still running, the Father was already running toward you.
If you follow Jesus, you are called to go after the lost — just like the Shepherd, just like the woman with the lamp. Who is the one person in your life who is still lost? That name in your mind right now is not a coincidence. You are not called to save them — that is God's job. You are called to go.

  1. Have you ever thought of God as distant, disappointed, or hard to reach? How does Luke 15 change or challenge that picture?
  2. The sheep wandered. The coin was dropped. Which story feels more like your own — and why?
  3. What does it mean to you personally that the father was already running before his son arrived home?
  4. Who is your 'one'? Who is the person in your life the Holy Spirit keeps bringing to mind?
  5. What is one step you could take this week to go after your one?
  1. God is the pursuer — you are here because He never stopped looking for you. (Luke 19:10)
  2. You are never just a number — heaven counts names, not crowds. (Luke 15:7)
  3. Grace carries you home — the sheep contributed nothing to its own rescue. (Ephesians 2:4–5)
  4. Your value is unshaken — you were made in the image of God and nothing can erase that. (Genesis 1:27)
  5. The Father is already running — while you were still a long way off, He saw you. (Romans 5:8)
  • Three parables. One message. One heartbeat: God loves lost people and goes after them.
  • Luke 15 is often called the greatest chapter ever written about the love of God.
  • The Pharisees muttered because Jesus welcomed sinners. Jesus responded not with an argument — but with a story.
  • Holiness doesn't avoid darkness — it invades it. Jesus wasn't contaminated by sinners; sinners were transformed by Jesus.
